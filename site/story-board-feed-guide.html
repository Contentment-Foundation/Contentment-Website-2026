How to feed stories & media to the Story Board

A practical guide for adding educator and student voices — photos, quotes, and video — using Google Drive and YouTube today, with a staff-friendly admin portal planned next.

Two phases

Phase 1 — Now Collect media in Google Drive and YouTube. A technical teammate adds story entries to one shared data file. Changes appear on the Story Board and world map after deploy.
Phase 2 — Coming A web admin portal so program and comms staff can add, preview, and publish stories without editing code — with photo upload, link import, and a review step before go-live.

Google Drive — photos

1. Organize your folder

Create a shared Drive folder, e.g. Contentment — Story Board Media, with subfolders for Educators and Students. Use clear filenames: kenya-aisha-teacher.jpg.

2. Share the image

  1. Upload the photo.
  2. Right-click → Share → General access: Anyone with the link → Viewer.
  3. Copy the link. It looks like:
    https://drive.google.com/file/d/1ABC…xyz/view?usp=sharing

3. Paste it into the Pin form — no manual conversion needed

Paste that share link straight into the Photo — Google Drive link field on Story Board's Pin/Edit form. It extracts the file ID and builds the direct preview link for you automatically, and shows a live preview right there so you can confirm it worked before saving. You don't need to hand-edit the URL yourself.

Only if you're hand-editing program-data.js directly (developers, not content contributors): the direct format is https://drive.google.com/thumbnail?id=FILE_ID&sz=w1000 — Drive's own thumbnail service, not the older uc?export=view trick. The old format is meant for downloading the original file and often fails to hotlink (a "can't scan this file for viruses" page instead of the image, or intermittent throttling), especially on larger photos.

If the image still doesn't show

  • Confirm sharing is Anyone with the link — this is the most common cause
  • Try re-sharing and re-copying the link — Drive occasionally issues a fresh link with a different ID
  • Use a JPG under ~500 KB if loading is slow

Be aware: Google Drive isn't an official image CDN, and hotlinking a Drive file on a public website is best-effort — Google can still intermittently rate-limit or block repeated external requests to a file even when it's correctly shared and correctly formatted. This isn't something our conversion step can fix; it's a Drive-side limitation. For any photo that needs to be reliably up for launch (not just a quick prototype pass), ask engineering to add it directly to site/assets/ instead — that's fully under our control and doesn't depend on Google's hotlinking behavior at all.

YouTube — video

Adding a clip

  1. Upload to the foundation YouTube channel (or an approved school channel).
  2. Set visibility to Public or Unlisted — not Private.
  3. Turn on captions for accessibility.
  4. Copy the watch URL, e.g. https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VIDEO_ID

Photo + video together

For the best card appearance, provide both a Google Drive photo (img) and a YouTube URL (video). The photo appears on the hanging card; clicking opens the full story and plays the video in the modal.

If you only have video, you can use YouTube’s thumbnail as the photo:
https://img.youtube.com/vi/VIDEO_ID/hqdefault.jpg

Step-by-step — add a story (Phase 1)

  1. Gather quote, full story, name/attribution, role, and country.
  2. Upload photo to Drive (and video to YouTube if applicable).
  3. Get the direct Drive image URL and YouTube watch URL.
  4. Send everything to the technical contact (see footer) or add to program-data.js under the correct country’s stories list.
  5. Preview on Story Board and map after deploy.

Example entry (for developers)

{
  q: "The curriculum changed how our whole school handles conflict.",
  by: "Maria S., Principal, Ohio",
  role: "educator",
  img: "https://drive.google.com/thumbnail?id=YOUR_FILE_ID&sz=w1000",
  cap: "Maria leading a restorative circle, 2025",
  video: "https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YOUR_VIDEO_ID",
  text: "Short teaser for the map popup…",
  full: "Full story text for the modal…"
}

Consent & quality checklist

Before any real name, face, or voice goes live:

  • Media release on file (especially for students under 18)
  • Name format approved by the school (first name + last initial is common)
  • No identifying details the family would not want public
  • Photo is respectful, well lit, and representative
  • Quote is accurate and approved by the speaker or school lead
  • Video has captions; photo has a descriptive caption

Keep consent forms in a private Drive folder — never linked on the public site.

Phase 2 — admin portal (planned)

Non-technical staff will sign in to a simple web form:

  • Pick country, role, and write the quote + full story
  • Upload a photo or paste Google Drive / YouTube links
  • Preview exactly how the card and modal will look
  • Submit for review → approved stories publish automatically

The portal will store stories in a database and serve them to the website — no more hand-editing JSON. Until then, program-data.js is the source of truth.
