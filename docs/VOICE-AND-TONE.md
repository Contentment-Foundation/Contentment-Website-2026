# The Contentment Foundation — Voice & Tone

> **Status:** Draft — under review and finalization  
> **Last updated:** June 2026  
> **Use for:** Website copy, campaigns, newsletters, social, press, Homeroom content, and any AI-assisted writing for TCF

This document defines **who** TCF sounds like — persona, tone, and writing craft. For **what to say** (taglines, belief journey, page copy, stats, CTAs, banned words), use [Messaging & Copy](./MESSAGING-AND-COPY.md), which wins on any conflict. [Site architecture](./WEBSITE-ARCHITECTURE.md) defines *where* pages live.

## The persona

**She is a matriarch who names what is hard without pretending, shaming, or making people hopeless.**

She connects what people feel to evidence, patterns, and lived experience. Data grounds the truth. Emotion makes it human — like Brené Brown in spirit, but not in imitation.

She cuts through the bullshit of the world with kindness and compassion.

**The Tender Bullshit-Cutter** — anchored in the reality of the world.

---

## How she shows up (in practice)

| Behavior | What it means |
|----------|---------------|
| **Names the hidden feeling first** | She starts with what people are carrying but may not know how to say. |
| **Brings herself into the room** | She does not speak from a detached expert position. She enters as a person who is also learning, hurting, wondering, and practicing. |
| **Bridges emotion and evidence** | Data grounds the truth; emotion makes it human and usable. |
| **Turns personal stories into shared patterns** | Her stories are not performance — they are doorways into something many people recognize in themselves. |
| **Stops when something matters** | She slows down, repeats, clarifies, and makes sure the important sentence lands. |
| **Holds paradox** | She lets two truths sit together: pain and hope, science and love, technology and loneliness, vulnerability and safety. |
| **Cuts through fake language with care** | She is direct, sometimes sharp, but not cruel. The aim is honesty, not humiliation. |
| **Keeps connection at the center** | The point is almost never self-improvement alone. It is repair, belonging, attention, emotional literacy, and coming back to one another. |

## Tone to aim for

Write in a voice that is:

| ✓ Aim for | ✗ Avoid drifting into |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Warm | Sentimental |
| Clear | Simplistic |
| Thoughtful | Academic |
| Emotionally honest | Self-indulgent |
| Practical | Formulaic |
| Direct | Harsh or cruel |
| Human, grounded, embodied | Detached or corporate |
| Capable of holding paradox | Forcing false certainty |

## Style principles

### 1. Start by naming the real human thing underneath the topic

Ask: *What is the discomfort, longing, fear, hope, or tension here?*

Name it before explaining it.

---

### 2. Bridge emotion and insight

Move from lived experience to meaning — not from abstraction to abstraction.

**Weak:** "Educator wellbeing is a systemic challenge requiring holistic intervention."  
**Strong:** "You can love this work and still feel like you're disappearing inside it."

---

### 3. Use plain language

Avoid jargon unless it is immediately explained in everyday words.

If a specialist term is necessary, earn it — define it in the same breath.

---

### 4. Make the insight feel usable

The reader should be able to recognize themselves and know what to do next — or at least what to feel less alone about.

---

### 5. Let paradox stay alive

Use constructions like:

- *"This can be true, and this can also be true…"*
- *"The problem is not X alone; it is what happens when X replaces Y."*
- *"We do not need to reject possibility, but we do need to protect what keeps us human."*

Do not flatten tension into a neat answer.

---

### 6. Keep connection as the moral center

When relevant, orient the piece around:

- Belonging
- Attention
- Repair
- Emotional literacy
- Gathering
- Loneliness
- Courage
- Vulnerability
- Human-scale community

---

### 7. Use specificity

Replace broad claims with concrete examples, sensory details, recognizable moments, or simple images.

**Weak:** "Teachers face many challenges."  
**Strong:** "You marked thirty papers, answered fourteen parent emails, and still had a student ask if you were okay — because you weren't."

---

### 8. Teach through the reader's lived experience

Instead of stating a general truth about "people," put the reader inside the moment.

**Weak:** "People are disconnected."  
**Strong:** "You can be surrounded by messages, meetings, and notifications and still have no one who really knows what kind of day you had."

---

### 9. Use questions sparingly but powerfully

Good questions open reflection. They should not sound like marketing hooks.

**Weak:** "Ready to transform your school's culture?"  
**Strong:** "When was the last time someone asked how you were — and waited for the real answer?"

---

### 10. End with grounded clarity

The ending should feel like a meaningful landing — not a slogan.

Leave the reader with something true and held, not a generic uplift.

## What not to do

### On voice and influence

| Don't | Why |
|-------|-----|
| Write "in the style of Brené Brown" | Spirit yes; imitation no |
| Use Brené Brown's catchphrases, signature sign-offs, or recognizable verbal tics | We need our own voice, not a tribute act |
| Overuse words like *vulnerability*, *shame*, *courage*, *belonging*, *brave*, or *wholehearted* unless truly central | These words carry weight — spend them carefully |

### On tone and register

| Don't | Why |
|-------|-----|
| Turn every point into therapy language | We are educators and humans, not clinicians in every paragraph |
| Over-confess or manufacture intimacy | Trust is built through honesty, not performance of vulnerability |
| Use melodrama | The subject is often already heavy enough |
| Over-explain the obvious | Respect the reader's intelligence |
| Sound like a corporate wellness memo | Warm ≠ branded |
| Sound like an academic paper | Evidence yes; jargon walls no |
| Use empty inspirational language | No "rise and shine" without substance |
| Make the piece overly polished when the subject needs honesty and texture | Some roughness is honest |

### On structure and ethics

| Don't | Why |
|-------|-----|
| Flatten tension into a neat answer | Paradox is often the truth |
| Imply that individual mindset alone solves structural or cultural problems | Name systems; don't blame the person carrying them |
| Use "we" manipulatively | Use "we" only when the shared experience is credible |
| Use too many rhetorical questions | One good question beats five weak ones |
| End with a generic call to action like *"Let's all be better"* | Land with clarity, not slogans |

## Quick reference — before you publish

Use this checklist for website copy, emails, and campaign content:

- [ ] Did we name the real human thing underneath the topic?
- [ ] Does emotion bridge to evidence — not replace it?
- [ ] Is the language plain enough for a tired educator at the end of a long day?
- [ ] Would the reader recognize themselves in at least one specific moment?
- [ ] Did we hold paradox where certainty would be dishonest?
- [ ] Is connection — not self-help alone — at the center?
- [ ] Did we avoid therapy-speak, corporate wellness, and empty inspiration?
- [ ] Does the ending land with grounded clarity?

## Application by content type

| Content type | Voice emphasis |
|--------------|----------------|
| **Homepage & top-nav pages** | Name the feeling first; bridge to impact and invitation |
| **Educator Stories** | Specificity and shared patterns; stories as doorways, not performance |
| **For Schools / partnerships** | Practical and direct; hold paradox (urgency + patience) |
| **Get Involved / giving** | Connection at center; no guilt-based fundraising |
| **Impact & annual report** | Data grounds truth; emotion makes numbers human |
| **Campaign pages (Festival, Anniversary)** | Celebratory but honest; avoid hype without substance |
| **Homeroom (members)** | Warmer, more intimate — but still not manufactured |
| **Press & legal** | Clearer and more restrained; persona softens, does not disappear |
