AIO — AI Search Optimisation (GEO) · ChatGPT · Perplexity · Google AI Overviews · Bing Copilot

Factual density

Lead every page with concrete numbers: 325 schools, 86%, 12 countries, 11,925 educators. AI engines extract and cite statistics — put them in the first two sentences.

Direct answers

Write one-sentence answers before expanding. "Teacher wellbeing improves classroom quality. In the largest RCT of its kind…" — AI pulls the first sentence as a direct answer.

Named citations

Reference Jennings et al. (2017) and Durlak et al. (2011) by name with DOI links. AI engines trust pages that cite peer-reviewed research and surface them as authoritative sources.

Organisation entity

Consistent name, URL, founding date, and mission across site, social profiles, Wikidata, and press mentions. AI engines rely on entity recognition to understand who TCF is.

FAQ format

FAQPage schema + visible Q&A blocks on /give/monthly and /why. AI engines pull FAQ answers directly into overviews — this is the fastest path to AI search visibility.

Question-shaped headings

Use descriptive H2s that mirror likely queries: "Does teacher wellbeing improve student outcomes?" "How does TCF deliver its programme?" AI engines match headings to user questions.
