## Ready-to-use copy lines

Use these as written unless legal or comms approves a variation. Always pair a public claim with the citation below.

### Line 1 · Teacher wellbeing improves classroom quality

**Copy:**

> Teacher wellbeing improves classroom quality. In the largest randomized trial of its kind, supporting teachers' wellbeing improved the emotional quality of their classroom interactions. (Jennings et al., 2017.)

**Best citation pairing:** Use **both** Jennings sources below — 2009 for the theoretical framework, 2017 for the randomized trial proof. Strongest when you need credibility *and* evidence.

| Use on | Audience |
|--------|----------|
| Why Teacher Wellbeing | Future members (Step 3: trust) |
| For Schools / school leader content | Leaders who need research before conversation |
| Home (proof band) | Brief stat with link to Why page |

### Line 2 · Wellbeing supports achievement, not just behavior

**Copy (suggested):**

> Wellbeing is a path to achievement, not a detour from it. A meta-analysis of school-based social and emotional learning programs found an 11-percentile-point gain in academic achievement across 213 programs and more than 270,000 students. (Durlak et al., 2011.)

**Optional follow-up (persistence):** Gains in academic performance and wellbeing persist over time. (Taylor et al., 2017.)

| Use on | Audience |
|--------|----------|
| For Schools | School leaders held accountable for outcomes |
| Why Teacher Wellbeing | "But what about" cards, achievement framing |
| Messaging to leaders (per Tim) | Wellbeing → social-emotional thriving → academic outcomes |

## Source 1 · Teacher wellbeing and classroom quality (Jennings, UVA)

Two papers carry this claim. **Using both is strongest:** one establishes the framework, one is the randomized trial that proves it.

### Jennings & Greenberg (2009) — The framework

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Citation** | Jennings, P. A., & Greenberg, M. T. (2009). The Prosocial Classroom: Teacher Social and Emotional Competence in Relation to Student and Classroom Outcomes. *Review of Educational Research*, *79*(1), 491–525. |
| **Role** | Highly cited theoretical framework linking teacher social-emotional competence to student and classroom outcomes |
| **Link** | [SAGE Journals](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.3102/0034654308325693) |
| **DOI** | [10.3102/0034654308325693](https://doi.org/10.3102/0034654308325693) |

### Jennings et al. (2017) — The proof (CARE for Teachers RCT)

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Citation** | Jennings, P. A., Brown, J. L., Frank, J. L., Doyle, S., Oh, Y., Davis, R., Rasheed, D., DeWeese, A., Rasheed, M., & Greenberg, M. T. (2017). Impacts of the CARE for Teachers Program on Teachers' Social and Emotional Competence and Classroom Interactions. *Journal of Educational Psychology*, *109*(7), 1010–1028. |
| **Finding** | Supporting teachers' wellbeing measurably improved the emotional quality of their classroom interactions |
| **Role** | Largest randomized controlled trial framing for the classroom-quality claim |
| **DOI** | [10.1037/edu0000187](https://doi.org/10.1037/edu0000187) |
| **Free PDF** | [Garrison Institute — CARE for Teachers research](https://www.garrisoninstitute.org/resources/care-for-teachers-research/) |

**On-brand note:** TCF materials already cite Jennings. These sources align with research the Foundation has used — we are standing on evidence we know, not borrowing unfamiliar citations.

## Source 2 · Wellbeing supports achievement (Durlak meta-analysis)

### Durlak et al. (2011) — Gold-standard achievement stat

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Citation** | Durlak, J. A., Weissberg, R. P., Dymnicki, A. B., Taylor, R. D., & Schellinger, K. B. (2011). The Impact of Enhancing Students' Social and Emotional Learning: A Meta-Analysis of School-Based Universal Interventions. *Child Development*, *82*(1), 405–432. |
| **Finding** | Across 213 programs and 270,000+ students, participants showed an **11-percentile-point gain** in academic achievement |
| **Role** | Primary citation for "wellbeing supports achievement" messaging to school leaders |
| **DOI** | [10.1111/j.1467-8624.2010.01564.x](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1467-8624.2010.01564.x) |
| **Links** | [Wiley](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/j.1467-8624.2010.01564.x) · [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/21291451/) · [CASEL PDF](https://casel.org/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/meta-analysis-child-development-1.pdf) |

### Taylor et al. (2017) — Optional: gains persist over time

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Citation** | Taylor, R. D., Oberle, E., Durlak, J. A., & Weissberg, R. P. (2017). Promoting Positive Youth Development Through School-Based Social and Emotional Learning Interventions: A Meta-Analysis of Follow-Up Effects. *Child Development*, *88*(4), 1156–1171. |
| **Finding** | Follow-up meta-analysis: academic and wellbeing gains from SEL interventions persist over time |
| **Role** | Strengthens leader-facing copy when durability matters ("this isn't a one-year fad") |
| **DOI** | [10.1111/cdev.12834](https://doi.org/10.1111/cdev.12834) |
| **Link** | [Wiley](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/cdev.12834) |

## Citation style on the website

| Context | Guidance |
|---------|----------|
| **Body copy** | Parenthetical: *(Jennings et al., 2017)* or linked footnote |
| **School leader pages** | Lead with outcome, then citation; link to DOI or CASEL PDF where helpful |
| **Footnotes / expandable "Sources"** | Full citation + link |
| **Social / short form** | "Research shows…" + link to Why page; avoid cramming DOIs into captions |

Do not over-cite in hero or emotional copy. Place evidence in trust sections (Step 3), proof bands, and leader-facing blocks.
